\subsection{Matched and cross-loss selective risk}

\input{Tables/main_results.tex}

Table~\ref{tab:main-results} shows an asymmetric, condition-dependent result.
Under nHD95 risk, nHD95-M32 has lower AURC than Dice-M32 in all ten conditions.
Under Dice risk, Dice-M32 is lower in six conditions---all four Pet conditions
and the two target-adapted Kvasir-SEG conditions---whereas nHD95-M32 is lower
for Kvasir-SEG CLIPSeg-General and all three FIVES conditions. Thus boundary
matching is consistent in this benchmark, but matching is not a universal
ordering principle.

SDC is also a substantive competitor: it has the lowest Dice-risk AURC among
the five primary methods in six of ten conditions. Dice-M32 is lowest in two,
negative entropy in one, and nHD95-M32 in one. The complete \(2\times2\)
matched and cross-loss decomposition is reported in
Table~\ref{tab:cross-loss-results}.

\subsection{Quadrature-resolution ablation}

Across the 20 matched score--risk series in
Table~\ref{tab:quadrature-ablation}, the absolute AURC difference between
\(M=8\) and \(M=32\) has median \(0.0011\) and maximum \(0.0118\). For
\(M=2\) versus \(M=32\), these values increase to \(0.0061\) and \(0.0515\).
The ranking metric is not monotone in quadrature resolution: \(M=8\) has lower
AURC than \(M=32\) in one of ten Dice series and three of ten nHD95 series,
while \(M=2\) does so in five and two, respectively. We therefore treat
\(M=32\) as the fixed primary numerical resolution, not as an empirically
guaranteed AURC optimum.

\subsection{Paired uncertainty and multiplicity control}

Table~\ref{tab:statistical-tests} reports all 20 signed estimates
\(\Delta=\AURC(\text{Dice-M32})-\AURC(\text{nHD95-M32})\), whose observed
values range from \(-0.0181\) to \(0.1615\). Each cell retains its 95\% paired
image-cluster percentile-bootstrap interval, raw two-sided bootstrap tail
\(p\)-value, and Holm-adjusted \(p\)-value. The pointwise intervals exclude zero
in 14 of 20 rows; 13 corresponding tests remain below \(0.05\) after adjustment.
We report every row without significance filtering; the effect signs and
magnitudes above are not replaced by this binary summary. A pointwise interval
and a family-adjusted \(p\)-value answer different multiplicity questions.

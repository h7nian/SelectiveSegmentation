\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The theory predicts that geometry can change the safest ordering, not that a
matched score must always win. We therefore read the results as two predeclared
adjacent steps. Throughout,
\(\Delta=\AURC(\text{left})-\AURC(\text{right})\), so negative values favor the
left score. Raw AURC is primary; normalized AURC supplies condition-relative
context.

\paragraph{Region overlap to worst-case boundary.}
Among the ten target-adapted conditions, nHD-M32 has lower nHD-risk AURC than
Dice-M32 in all ten. Every paired interval excludes zero, and every
Holm-transformed tail probability is below .05. Under Dice risk, the direction
reverses in eight of ten conditions, but only three matched-direction target
comparisons retain a transformed value below .05. Across all sixteen fixed
conditions, nHD wins its matched nHD comparison in 15, while Dice wins its
matched Dice comparison in 10; the remaining counts are respectively one
degenerate tie, and five nHD wins plus that tie. Thus the clearest empirical
separation is worst-boundary risk, not a symmetric same-name advantage.

\paragraph{Worst-case to robust boundary.}
Comparing only the two boundary scores, nHD-M32 is lower under nHD risk in nine
of ten target-adapted conditions, and nHD95-M32 is lower under nHD95 risk in
eight. Over all sixteen conditions the corresponding counts are 11 and 12,
with one degenerate tie in each comparison. Yet only two and one target-adapted
matched-direction contrasts, respectively, retain a Holm-transformed value
below .05. The percentile extension therefore changes rankings, but its gains
are smaller and condition dependent; this experiment is not evidence that the
nHD Wasserstein corollary transfers to nHD95.

\paragraph{Baselines, quadrature, and complete panels.}
Among all seventeen fixed scores, a boundary-indexed score attains the lowest
raw AURC in nine of ten target-adapted conditions under each boundary risk; SDC
on FIVES/CLIPSeg-Target is the exception in both cases. SDC and foreground
entropy remain highly competitive under Dice risk, reinforcing that the
framework supplies loss-specific candidates rather than a universal winner.
For Dice-M32 versus Dice-Exact on the ten target-adapted conditions, Spearman
\(\rho\geq.9992\), Kendall \(\tau_b\geq.9791\), mean per-image score error is at
most .0031, and the absolute Dice-risk AURC difference is at most .0037.

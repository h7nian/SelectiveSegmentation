\paragraph{Worst-boundary risk.}
nHD-M32 lowers nHD-risk AURC relative to Dice-M32 in all ten target conditions;
all ten pointwise intervals exclude zero. A boundary-indexed score is lowest
among the six displayed methods in nine conditions, with SDC on
FIVES/CLIPSeg-Target the exception. nHD-M32 also has higher association with
realized nHD safety than Dice-M32 in all ten, with Spearman differences
.179--.432. The level-set score thus carries spatial-extreme information absent
from its regional counterpart.

\paragraph{Robust boundary risk.}
nHD-M32 is lower than nHD95-M32 in nine conditions under nHD risk; nHD95-M32 is
lower in eight under nHD95 risk. Only four and two matched-direction intervals
exclude zero. The latter comparison is checkpoint-sensitive: five conditions
reverse across seeds and seed 0 disagrees with the majority in three. Both
boundary macro contrasts also reverse at action threshold \(\gamma=.3\). nHD95
is therefore a useful but condition-sensitive robust endpoint, not a guaranteed
improvement over nHD.

\paragraph{Regional contrast.}
Dice-M32 is lower than nHD-M32 in eight conditions under Dice risk, but only
three intervals exclude zero; SDC and foreground entropy remain competitive.
Outside FIVES/CLIPSeg-Target, Dice-M32 and foreground entropy correlate
.916--.960. Dice-M32 and Dice-Exact have \(\rho\geq.9992\),
\(\tau_b\geq.9791\), and at most .363 displayed AURC difference, so numerical
integration is not the limiting issue.

Neither richer single-map coupling nor checkpoint disagreement supplies a new
Dice winner. Two-block gains are only .0195/.0020 mean/median; no component-grid
variant passes its fixed gate or beats SDC or foreground entropy in more than
6/10 conditions. Three-checkpoint Ensemble-Q also loses to LevelSet-Q in 9/10.
These negative ablations support the count-law explanation.

\paragraph{Diagnostics.}
nHD/nHD95 M32--M128 Spearman agreement is .973--.998/.951--.996. Known-posterior
tests show TV near one with Dice score error only .0135--.0393, while support
checks find incompatible references on up to 11.5\% of FIVES and 15.4\% of
ISIC. The nHD ranking result is strong, but one annotation per image cannot
validate a nested conditional posterior. Appendix~\ref{app:additional-results}
reports all AURCs, intervals, ablations, curves, and stress tests.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The theory allows geometry to change the safest ordering; it does not promise
that a matched score always wins. We follow the two fixed adjacent steps below.
Displayed AURC-derived scalars are multiplied by \(100\), whereas nAURC is not;
for \(\Delta=\AURC(\text{left})-\AURC(\text{right})\), negative favors the left.

\paragraph{Region overlap to worst-case boundary.}
nHD-M32 lowers nHD-risk AURC relative to Dice-M32 in all ten target conditions,
with every pointwise paired-bootstrap interval excluding zero. Under Dice risk,
Dice-M32 is lower in eight, but only three corresponding intervals exclude
zero. The strongest separation is therefore worst-boundary risk, not a
symmetric same-name advantage.

\paragraph{Worst-case to robust boundary.}
nHD-M32 is lower than nHD95-M32 in nine target conditions under nHD risk;
nHD95-M32 is lower in eight under nHD95 risk. Only four and two matched-direction
intervals, respectively, exclude zero. Replacing a maximum by a percentile does
change rankings, but the gains are smaller, condition dependent, and do not
transfer the nHD Wasserstein corollary to nHD95.

\paragraph{Baselines and numerical fidelity.}
A boundary-indexed score has the lowest raw AURC in nine of ten target
conditions under each boundary risk; SDC on FIVES/CLIPSeg-Target is the
exception. SDC and foreground entropy remain competitive for Dice, so the
framework supplies loss-specific candidates rather than a universal winner.
Dice-M32 and Dice-Exact have \(\rho\geq.9992\), \(\tau_b\geq.9791\), and at most
.370 displayed Dice-risk AURC difference, without changing either
region--boundary directional count. For nHD/nHD95, M32--M128 Spearman \(\rho\) is
.973--.998/.951--.996 and the largest displayed matched-risk gaps are .456/.324;
M32 is adequate for the common-budget comparison but not numerically exact.

\paragraph{Stress tests.}
In the 360-cell known-posterior study, the correctly specified coupling has zero
posterior discrepancy and M32 mean absolute error .0084--.0087; under
misspecification, mask-law TV is near one while Dice error remains
.0135--.0393. This shows why TV can be too blunt and motivates loss-matched
geometry, without validating \(Q_p\) on real data. Exact cardinality checks find
zero support for up to 11.5\% of FIVES and 15.4\% of ISIC target references.
The action-sensitivity warning also fires: both boundary macro contrasts reverse
at \(\gamma=.3\), while the two Dice--nHD macro directions persist. Thus the
region-to-worst-boundary result is more stable than the worst-to-robust step.
Finally, the balanced runtime ladder gives 11.9--468.6, 32.3--1261.4, and
102.3--4628.8 ms/image for joint three-loss M2, M8, and M32, versus
13.2--353.5 for Dice-Exact alone; these hardware measurements compare unequal
outputs.

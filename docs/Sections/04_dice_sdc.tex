\section{Geometric loss instantiations and relation to SDC}
\label{sec:theory}

For binary masks, define
\begin{equation*}
  \Dice(A,B)=\frac{2|A\cap B|}{|A|+|B|},
\end{equation*}
with \(\Dice(\varnothing,\varnothing)=1\) and Dice equal to zero when
exactly one mask is empty. The loss \(L_{\Dice}=1-\Dice\) lies in
\([0,1]\). Let \(d_J\) be Jaccard distance with
\(d_J(\varnothing,\varnothing)=0\), and let \(W_{1,d}\) denote the corresponding
1-Wasserstein transport cost. Appendix~\ref{app:theory} proves
\begin{equation*}
  \Delta_{L_{\Dice},\Yhat}(x)
  \leq\min\{\TV(P_x,Q_{p(x)}),2W_{1,d_J}(P_x,Q_{p(x)})\}.
\end{equation*}
This permits distinct masks that are geometrically close even though their point
masses have TV one; the factor two means it is not uniformly tighter than TV.

Equation~\eqref{eq:quad} gives
\begin{align*}
  C_{\Dice,M}(x)
  &:=-\sum_{m=1}^M w_m
      \left[1-\Dice(Y_{t_m},\Yhat)\right], \\
  1+C_{\Dice,M}(x)
  &=\sum_{m=1}^M w_m\Dice(Y_{t_m},\Yhat).
\end{align*}
Thus \(1+C_{\Dice,M}\) estimates expected Dice under \(Q_p\) and is
rank-equivalent to the loss-oriented confidence.

Because level sets change only at probability knots, sorting tied groups gives
exact Dice confidence in \(O(N\log N)\) time and removes quadrature error.
Appendix~\ref{app:theory} gives the sum, pseudocode, and all endpoint and empty
cases; midpoint quadrature remains the common estimator for arbitrary losses.

\paragraph{Full surface Hausdorff.}
Normalized penalized full surface-set Hausdorff is a metric on digital surfaces
and a pseudometric on masks. Reverse triangle gives the constant-one bound
\(\Delta_{L^{\mathrm{nHD}},\Yhat}(x)
\leq W_{1,d_{H,x}}(P_x,Q_{p(x)})
\leq\TV(P_x,Q_{p(x)})\), where the appendix defines the normalized surface cost
\(d_{H,x}\), diagonal and empty penalties, and a quantile jump showing why pooled
nHD95 does not inherit this result.

When the denominator is positive, SDC computes the soft Dice between \(p\)
and the deployed mask:
\begin{equation}
  \SDC(p,\Yhat)
  =
  \frac{2\sum_i p_i\hat y_i}
       {\sum_i p_i+\sum_i\hat y_i},
  \qquad
  \hat y_i=\mathbf 1\{i\in\Yhat\}.
  \label{eq:sdc}
\end{equation}
For any mask law \(Q\) with marginals \(\Ex_QY_i=p_i\), let
\(N(Y)=2\sum_iY_i\hat y_i\) and \(D(Y)=\sum_iY_i+\sum_i\hat y_i\).
Linearity gives \(\SDC=\Ex_QN(Y)/\Ex_QD(Y)\), whereas expected Dice is
\(\Ex_Q[N(Y)/D(Y)]\); the two are unequal in general. For example, with
\(p=(1/2,1/2)\) and \(\Yhat=\{1\}\), independent pixels, the level-set
coupling, and SDC give \(5/12\), \(1/3\), and \(1/2\), respectively.
Appendix~\ref{app:theory} proves the identity and handles empty masks.

The assumptions are not ordered. Published SDC theory relates its
coupling-invariant ratio to a product-Bernoulli Dice target; identifying that
target with true expected Dice additionally requires exact pointwise marginals
and conditional pixel independence \citep{sdc}. Our estimator is exact instead
under the generally dependent, comonotone \(Q_p\), and approaches true ideal
confidence only when Equation~\eqref{eq:loss-discrepancy} is small. Thus the
framework extends loss indexing beyond Dice, but neither method is a special
case of the other nor uniformly less assumptive.

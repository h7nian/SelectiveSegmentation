\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

A segmentation can cover almost the right region yet place one component far
from the anatomy. When difficult cases may be deferred to a human, confidence
should detect the failure that actually governs deployment. Dice, full
Hausdorff distance (HD), and HD95 encode different harms; no uncertainty average
specifies which one deserves rejection.

For a fixed deployed mask \(\Yhat(x)\) and loss \(L\), we therefore target
\[
  C_L^\star(x)=-\Ex[L(Y,\Yhat(x))\mid X=x].
\]
The target changes with both the loss and action. Yet even exact pixelwise
foreground marginals do not identify the joint mask law required by nonlinear
region and boundary losses.

We make one missing law explicit. Given a foreground map \(p\), draw a shared
threshold \(T\sim\operatorname{Unif}(0,1)\) and set
\(Y_T=\{i:p_i\geq T\}\). The nested candidates preserve \(p\) as their coverage
function \citep{goodman,molchanov} and sweeping the level produces coordinated
surface motion. Working-posterior risk becomes
\(\int_0^1L(Y_t,\Yhat)\,dt\). This is a tractable model, not a claim that the
unknown annotation posterior is nested.

Normalized penalized full-surface HD (nHD) is the cleanest instance. Its ground
cost is normalized surface-Hausdorff distance, so the reverse triangle inequality
turns posterior agreement in that geometry into score agreement. We then carry
score approximation error through selective risk to AURC regret. Pooled nHD95
provides a robust practical contrast but is nonmetric and does not inherit that
corollary. Dice supplies a regional contrast: for a fixed action, posterior Dice
depends on two counts, explaining why the coupling-invariant SDC ratio can be
difficult to improve from one map.

We freeze every probability map and action, then change only confidence and
evaluation loss. The primary question is whether boundary-aligned confidence
improves selection under worst-boundary risk. We next compare full and robust
boundary loss, then use Dice to expose where the shared-threshold coupling is
less informative. Five datasets, two model families, sixteen conditions, and a
complete \(3\times3\) score--risk design separate these questions.

\subsection{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Selective segmentation quality.}
Selective prediction evaluates confidence through risk--coverage
\citep{geifman2017selective,selectivenet,feng}. Segmentation quality control has
used reverse fitting, learned predictors, and spatial uncertainty summaries
\citep{valindria2017rca,values,zenk,guarino2026aggregation}. These approaches
may use atlases, validation labels, or learned features. We isolate the narrower
one-map setting, where the probability map and deployed mask are fixed.

\paragraph{Boundary geometry and mask sampling.}
Surface metrics expose errors that overlap can hide \citep{karimi2020}. Random
level sets are classical coverage-function constructions
\citep{goodman,molchanov} used in contour uncertainty
\citep{poethkow,bolin}. Dropout and ensembles instead provide multiple maps
\citep{gal2016dropout,lakshminarayanan2017ensembles}; agreement has been used by
Bayesian QuickNAT, while SAM compares masks after shifting its cutoff
\citep{roy2019bayesianquicknat,kirillov2023segmentanything}. We compare these
ideas at declared information budgets and never equate model disagreement with
annotation variability.

\paragraph{Dice confidence.}
SDC computes soft Dice between \(p\) and \(\Yhat\) \citep{sdc}. Level-set Dice
is an expectation of a ratio under an explicit coupling; SDC is a ratio of
expectations. Our count analysis and coupling ablations explain their empirical
proximity and make Dice a diagnostic contrast rather than the premise of the
framework.

\subsection{Our Contributions}

We contribute: (i) loss-indexed confidence and a shared-threshold estimator for
any bounded segmentation loss; (ii) an nHD flagship specialization linking
coherent boundary motion to a constant-one surface-Wasserstein bound; (iii) a
complete error chain from quadrature and posterior discrepancy to AURC regret,
with nHD95 and Dice specializations, Exact Dice, and an SDC count-dispersion
bound; and (iv) a locked five-dataset study with full cross-loss evaluation,
single-map baselines, paired uncertainty, action and checkpoint stress tests,
and separately labelled higher-budget posterior diagnostics.

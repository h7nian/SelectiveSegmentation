\begin{abstract}
Selective segmentation must decide both what mask to deploy and which predictions
to accept. We index confidence by the deployment loss: the ideal score is the
negative conditional risk of the fixed mask. Pixel marginals do not identify this
risk for nonlinear mask losses, so we state a shared-threshold level-set law as a
\emph{working posterior}; it turns expected structured loss into a one-dimensional
integral. We separate quadrature error from posterior discrepancy and carry score
error through to selective-risk and AURC regret. Dice and normalized Hausdorff
(nHD) instantiate the same Lipschitz--Wasserstein principle under Jaccard and
surface geometries; nonmetric nHD95 remains a robust bounded-loss extension, and
an exact Dice algorithm removes quadrature error. On five binary datasets and 16
fixed model--dataset conditions, a complete \(3\times3\) score--risk design finds
that nHD-M32 lowers nHD-risk AURC relative to Dice-M32 in all ten target conditions,
whereas Dice-M32 lowers Dice-risk AURC in eight. Across three checkpoints,
nHD versus nHD95 under nHD95 risk reverses direction in five target conditions,
with seed 0 outside the majority direction in three. Separately, fixed-threshold
sensitivity reverses both boundary macro contrasts at \(\gamma=.3\).
\end{abstract}

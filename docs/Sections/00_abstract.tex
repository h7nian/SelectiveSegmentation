\begin{abstract}
A mask can achieve high Dice overlap yet contain a small component dangerously
far from the anatomy. It may therefore be safe under a regional loss and unsafe
under a worst-boundary loss: \textbf{selective segmentation cannot rely on a
metric-agnostic confidence score}. A single probability map also supplies only
pixel marginals, not the joint mask law required by nonlinear deployment losses.
We address both gaps by fixing the deployed mask and loss, then declaring a
shared-threshold level-set law as a \emph{working posterior}. Its nested
candidates model coherent boundary motion, and their loss against the fixed
action yields a one-dimensional risk integral. Our theory separates numerical
quadrature from posterior discrepancy and propagates both to area under the
risk--coverage curve (AURC) regret. Normalized penalized full Hausdorff distance
(nHD) is the flagship loss: its surface geometry gives a constant-one
Wasserstein posterior bound. Normalized penalized HD95 (nHD95), which replaces
the maximum by the 95th percentile of pooled bidirectional surface distances,
is a robust nonmetric extension. Dice provides a regional contrast whose exact
algorithm and count-dispersion bound explain why level-set Dice and Soft Dice
Confidence (SDC) can behave similarly. Across five binary datasets and 16 frozen conditions, the
32-node nHD score lowers nHD-risk AURC relative to Dice-M32 in all ten target
conditions, with every pointwise interval excluding zero; a boundary-indexed
score is best among the displayed methods in nine. The nHD--nHD95 ordering is
smaller and sensitive to checkpoint and deployment threshold. \textbf{Reliable
selection requires both a declared loss and a working-posterior geometry matched
to how that loss measures error.}
\end{abstract}

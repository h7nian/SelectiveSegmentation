\begin{abstract}
Selective segmentation should reject masks that are unsafe under the deployment
loss. Boundary failures make this dependence visible: regional overlap can remain
high despite a dangerously displaced surface. We define confidence as the
negative conditional risk of the fixed deployed mask. Because pixel marginals do
not identify nonlinear mask risk, we declare a shared-threshold level-set law as
a \emph{working posterior}. Its nested candidates represent coherent boundary
motion and turn expected loss into a one-dimensional integral. We separate
quadrature from posterior error and propagate both to selective risk and area
under the risk--coverage curve (AURC) regret. Normalized penalized full
Hausdorff distance (nHD) is the flagship loss: its surface geometry gives a
constant-one Wasserstein posterior bound. Normalized penalized 95th-percentile
Hausdorff distance (nHD95) is a nonmetric robust extension; Dice is a regional
contrast whose exact algorithm and count-dispersion bound explain Soft Dice
Confidence (SDC). On five binary datasets and 16 frozen conditions, the 32-node
nHD score (nHD-M32) lowers nHD-risk AURC relative to Dice-M32 in all ten
target conditions, with every pointwise interval excluding zero; a
boundary-indexed score is best among the displayed methods in nine. The
nHD--nHD95 ordering is smaller and sensitive to checkpoint and deployment
threshold. Thus confidence needs both a target loss and a working-posterior
geometry matched to how that loss measures error.
\end{abstract}

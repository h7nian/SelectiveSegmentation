\section{Limitations and Conclusion}
\label{sec:limitations}
\label{sec:limitations-conclusion}

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The level-set law is one coupling compatible with a probability map, not a
posterior identified by calibrated marginals. Exact Dice removes its numerical
integration error, and larger \(M\) reduces midpoint error for the other losses,
but neither removes posterior discrepancy. Hence the AURC-regret bound neither
guarantees close-pair ranking nor a matched-score win. Cardinality diagnostics
expose support incompatibilities on FIVES and ISIC, but one label per image
cannot validate \(Q_p\).

Dice and normalized full HD share a Lipschitz--Wasserstein proof template, but
their Jaccard and surface-Hausdorff conditions are not interchangeable. Pooled
nHD95 is nonmetric and does not inherit the HD corollary; it remains covered by
the bounded-loss, posterior-discrepancy, and AURC theory.

The boundary surface, pixel spacing, diagonal normalization, percentile pooling,
and empty-mask penalty are substantive deployment choices. Our experiment uses
native pixels and one convention. The design covers five binary datasets and two
model families. The prespecified rules mark nHD versus nHD95 under nHD95 risk as
training-sensitive and the same geometric step as threshold-sensitive. Three
checkpoints are descriptive, while the seed-0 image bootstrap excludes training,
site, multi-rater, and patient-cluster uncertainty. All primary methods use one
probability map. A three-checkpoint auxiliary compares a mean-map level-set law
with an empirical member-mask law at a higher inference budget; it is not
evidence that either law equals the annotation posterior. Learned failure
detectors, multiclass actions, and absolute risk calibration remain outside
scope.
Appendix~\ref{app:limitations} expands these constraints.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
\label{sec:conclusion}
Selective segmentation begins with a practical choice: which failure matters
when a mask is accepted? Loss indexing turns that choice into an explicit
confidence target. The shared-threshold working posterior makes the otherwise
hidden joint assumption inspectable; the theory traces its error to selective
risk and AURC; and the losses move from overlap to worst-case and robust boundary
error. The framework compares these rankings without promising one universal
geometry or score.

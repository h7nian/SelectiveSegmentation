\section{Experimental protocol}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Five datasets, sixteen frozen conditions}

We study five native-binary tasks: Oxford-IIIT Pet, Kvasir-SEG polyps, FIVES
retinal vessels, ISIC 2018 skin lesions, and TN3K thyroid nodules
\citep{parkhi2012cats,jha2020kvasir,jin2022fives,codella2019isic,
gong2023thyroid}. Official splits are used except for Kvasir, whose 800/200
split is fixed by SHA-256 order. Empty references remain in the cohort.

Each dataset has \textsc{CLIPSeg-General}, \textsc{CLIPSeg-Target}, and
\textsc{DeepLabV3-Target}; Pet additionally has a released
\textsc{DeepLabV3-External} cat/dog condition, giving sixteen conditions.
Target models follow one frozen 40-epoch schedule and seed 0. No test label
selects a checkpoint, hyperparameter, or condition. Within a condition every
score receives the same frozen native-resolution probability map and action,
isolating ranking from segmentation quality. Appendix~\ref{app:benchmark-protocol}
specifies cohorts, prompts, training, checkpoints, and integrity checks.

\subsection{One action, three geometric losses}

The deployed action is fixed once,
\begin{equation*}
  \Yhat(x)=\{i:p_i(x)\geq0.5\}.
\end{equation*}
An integration threshold changes only a candidate mask. Besides
\(L_{\Dice}=1-\Dice\), let \(D(A,B)\) pool both directed nearest distances
between inner digital surfaces. On an \(H\times W\) native grid with
\(D_x=\sqrt{H^2+W^2}\),
\begin{equation*}
  L_{\mathrm{nHD},x}=\frac{\max D(A,B)}{D_x},
  \qquad
  L_{\mathrm{nHD95},x}=\frac{Q_{0.95}(D(A,B))}{D_x}.
\end{equation*}
The percentile follows pooling. Empty--empty costs zero and a one-sided empty
pair costs one, so all primary risks lie in \([0,1]\). Raw native-pixel HD and
HD95 are retained descriptively; Appendix~\ref{app:benchmark-protocol} fixes
surface, quantile, resizing, and empty-mask conventions.

\subsection{Scores and the complete cross-loss design}

For each of the three losses we compute midpoint confidence
\begin{equation*}
  C_{L,M}(x)=-\frac1M\sum_{m=1}^M
  L\!\left(\{i:p_i(x)\geq(m-1/2)/M\},\Yhat(x)\right)
\end{equation*}
at \(M\in\{2,8,32\}\); the three \(M=32\) scores are primary. Dice-Exact
is the probability-knot oracle from Corollary~\ref{app:cor:exact-dice}.
Seven single-map references---SDC, mean maximum probability, mean negative
entropy, QFR-Entropy, PLM-10/PLA-10-Entropy, MMMC-Entropy, and foreground
entropy---complete seventeen scores \citep{sdc,guarino2026aggregation,
holder2021uncertainty}.

Dice-M32, nHD-M32, and nHD95-M32 are evaluated under all three risks, producing
the full \(3\times3\) indexed-score matrix. The only predeclared primary contrasts are
the two adjacent geometric steps under their neighboring risks:
\begin{equation*}
\begin{aligned}
  &\AURC(\text{Dice-M32})-\AURC(\text{nHD-M32})
    &&\text{under Dice and nHD},\\
  &\AURC(\text{nHD-M32})-\AURC(\text{nHD95-M32})
    &&\text{under nHD and nHD95}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation*}
All nonadjacent and mismatched cells are descriptive. The \(M\)-ladder and
Dice-Exact additionally separate quadrature error from posterior discrepancy
using direct score errors and rank agreement.

\subsection{Locked analysis and paired inference}

One immutable manifest per condition binds ordered samples, probability maps,
labels, checkpoints, source fingerprints, and hashes. Independent Slurm jobs
freeze each condition, compute common quantities, compute each
\((\text{condition},M)\) score shard, and strictly assemble only matching
identities. A separate locked diagnostic reports marginal Brier/ECE, empty-mask
rates, and level-set ladder diversity; none validates the joint posterior or
tunes a method.

We compute tie-aware empirical AURC as the exact expectation over uniform
ordering within score ties; lower is better. Each of the four contrasts per
condition receives a 95\% pointwise interval and approximate two-sided tail
probability from 10,000 paired image-cluster bootstrap resamples
\citep{efron1993}. Holm transforms are applied separately to the predeclared
40-comparison Pet/Kvasir/FIVES core and 24-comparison ISIC/TN3K extension
families \citep{holm1979}. We treat them as multiplicity-aware evidence
summaries, not exact finite-sample family-wise-error guarantees. Appendix
\ref{app:benchmark-protocol} gives every score, diagnostic, AURC, bootstrap,
and multiplicity convention.

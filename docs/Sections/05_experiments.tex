\section{Experimental protocol}
\label{sec:experiments}

\noindent\textbf{Frozen conditions.}
We evaluate Oxford-IIIT Pet, Kvasir-SEG, Fundus Image Vessel Segmentation
(FIVES), International Skin Imaging Collaboration 2018 (ISIC 2018), and
Thyroid Nodule 3K (TN3K)
\citep{parkhi2012cats,jha2020kvasir,jin2022fives,codella2019isic,
gong2023thyroid}. Each has the Contrastive Language--Image Pretraining-based
segmentation model CLIPSeg in general and target-adapted forms
(\textsc{CLIPSeg-General} and \textsc{CLIPSeg-Target}), plus
\textsc{DeepLabV3-Target}; Pet also has a released
\textsc{DeepLabV3-External} model, giving sixteen conditions. Headline counts
use the \textbf{ten target conditions} trained for 40 epochs at seed 0. Seeds 1--2 form a
separate descriptive extension and never select a checkpoint. Every method in a
condition receives the same native-resolution map and action. Empty references
remain. \Cref{app:benchmark-protocol} fixes splits, training, cohorts,
and provenance.

\noindent\textbf{Architecture and domain extension.}
We do not replace conditions after seeing their results. Instead, a separately
locked extension asks whether the conclusions survive a different probability-map
family and a new visual domain. We target-adapt SegFormer-B2
\citep{xie2021segformer} on each of the five original datasets, pairing it with
the existing DeepLabV3 target condition. We then add the Diverse Unsupervised
Saliency (DUTS) benchmark \citep{wang2017duts}, training both SegFormer-B2 and
DeepLabV3 on its 10,553-image training split and evaluating the fixed 5,019-image
test split. These seven new conditions retain the same 40-epoch, seed-0 protocol,
form their own multiplicity family, and are never pooled into the primary
sixteen-condition analysis.

\noindent\textbf{One action and three losses.}
We fix \(\Yhat=\{i:p_i\geq0.5\}\). Integration thresholds change candidates,
not this action. Besides \(1-\Dice\), let \(D(A,B)\) pool both directed nearest
distances between inner digital surfaces. For image diagonal
\(D_x=\sqrt{H^2+W^2}\),
\begin{equation*}
  L_{\mathrm{nHD},x}=\frac{\max D(A,B)}{D_x},\qquad
  L_{\mathrm{nHD95},x}=\frac{Q_{0.95}(D(A,B))}{D_x}.
\end{equation*}
Empty--empty costs zero and a one-sided empty pair costs one. nHD is the
flagship worst-surface endpoint; nHD95 suppresses its extreme five-percent tail;
Dice is the regional contrast.

\noindent\textbf{Scores and comparisons.}
For each loss,
\begin{equation*}
  C_{L,M}=-\frac1M\sum_{m=1}^M
  L\!\left(\{i:p_i\geq(m-1/2)/M\},\Yhat\right),
  \qquad M\in\{2,8,32\}.
\end{equation*}
Here \(M\) is the number of midpoint thresholds. The \textbf{M32 scores are
primary} and Dice-Exact is the knot oracle. Seven single-map
references---SDC, mean maximum probability, mean negative entropy, QFR-Entropy,
PLM/PLA-10-Entropy, MMMC-Entropy, and foreground entropy---give seventeen scores
\citep{sdc,guarino2026aggregation,holder2021uncertainty}. The
\textbf{complete \(3\times3\) matrix} is reported. The main contrast is nHD-M32 versus Dice-M32
under nHD risk; three fixed contrasts complete adjacent comparisons under Dice,
nHD, and nHD95 risk. All other cells are descriptive. M2/M8/M32, M128, and
Dice-Exact audit numerical sensitivity.

A higher-budget extension fixes the action of the three-checkpoint mean map and
compares its LevelSet-Q with the empirical law of checkpoint masks. Threshold-IoU,
member IoU/Dice, mutual information, and variance remain extension baselines
\citep{roy2019bayesianquicknat,kirillov2023segmentanything}.

\noindent\textbf{Locked analysis.}
Hashed manifests bind maps, labels, checkpoints, sample order, and each
one-task-per-job shard. Separate fixed studies examine posterior support,
known-posterior coupling, and action thresholds \(\gamma\in\{.3,.7\}\). Before
inspection, we declared a comparison sensitive when its macro sign changed or
at least three target conditions reversed; the primary \(\gamma=.5\), seed-0
analysis never changes.

We use \textbf{exact tie-aware AURC}. Displayed AURCs and contrasts are multiplied by
100; normalized excess AURC (nAURC) and computation remain unscaled. Each of 64 fixed condition-level
contrasts receives a pointwise 95\% interval from 10,000 paired image-bootstrap
resamples \citep{efron1993}. These are neither simultaneous tests nor inference
over training randomness. Fifteen-bin pixel expected calibration error (ECE)
and level-set ladder diversity are descriptive diagnostics, not tests of the
joint mask posterior. The hindsight oracle orders realized losses and is not
\(C_L^\star\).
